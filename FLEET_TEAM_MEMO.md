## What This Is

The Fault Advisor is an AI agent that helps diagnose and resolve Generac R2 system faults in plain language. You describe what you're seeing — no alarm codes required — and it walks you through what's wrong and how to fix it.

It's live now at **alarm.dash-ly.com** for internal testing before we roll it out to Field Pro dealers.

---

## How to Use It

**1. Pick your role** on the left sidebar before you start:
- **Installer / Dealer** — step-by-step technical guidance
- **Support / TSE** — full technical depth including internal notes and thresholds
- **Homeowner** — plain English, no jargon (test this too — this is what a homeowner would see)

**2. Describe the problem in plain English.** You don't need to know the alarm code. Examples:
- "Battery keeps shutting off during backup mode"
- "Self-test failed on a new install, what do I check first?"
- "Alarm 0xB050 on the Gateway, what does that mean?"

**3. Have a conversation.** The agent asks follow-up questions. Answer them like you would answer a colleague. The more context you give, the more specific the guidance gets.

**4. Rate every response** with 👍 or 👎. This is not optional — it is the most important thing you can do.

---

## Why Your Feedback Matters More Than You Think

Every 👍 you give on a correct answer gets used to make the agent smarter for the next person with the same problem.

Here's what happens over time:

- **Today:** Dealer asks about a battery shutdown → agent takes 15 exchanges to get to the right answer
- **In 30 days:** Agent has seen that pattern from real conversations → gets there in 4 exchanges
- **In 6 months:** Dealers get first-call-resolution answers on the most common faults without going back and forth

The agent learns from the conversations that worked. No one has to manually update anything. The 👍 is the training signal.

---

## What We Need From You During Testing

1. **Use it on real problems** — don't just ask test questions. The more realistic the scenario, the more useful the data.
2. **Rate every response** — 👍 if it helped, 👎 if it was wrong or incomplete.
3. **Try all three roles** — especially Homeowner, since that tier needs the most refinement before we can give it to customers.
4. **Note anything that's factually wrong** — if the agent describes the wrong cable type, wrong steps, or wrong device, flag it to Benyamin directly so it can be corrected in the knowledge base.

## The Bigger Picture

Phase 1 goal: Internal fleet team uses this daily and the agent gets measurably better each week based on real feedback.

Phase 2 goal: Field Pro dealers use this on job sites. Fewer calls to TSE. Faster commissioning. Fewer repeat truck rolls.

Phase 3 goal: A lighter version for homeowners. They describe what they see, get plain-English guidance, and only call their installer when truly necessary.

The quality of this tool in Phase 3 depends entirely on the quality of feedback we collect in Phase 1. What the fleet team puts in now directly determines what homeowners get later.
